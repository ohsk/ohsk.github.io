Flocktracker

Designing a lightweight, phone-based survey application for capturing dynamic urban data

Project Date
2012 - Present
Role
UX Designer
Organization
MIT Department of Urban Studies & Planning, Mobility Futures Collaborative, & Urban Launchpad
Project Team
Daniel Heriberto Palencia Arreola, Kuan Butts, Albert Ching, Arturo Cadena, Muntasir Mamun

The Flocktracker Android-based application takes advantage of smartphones and tablets to enable data collection in the field, in a quick way, with high spatial and temporal resolution.

Originally designed for collecting data on minibus systems in megacities of the Global South, Flocktracker is suitable for a wide-range of field-based research initiatives. The project is the result of an ongoing collaborative effort under the research of MIT DUSP's Mobility Futures Collaborative. The current application is based off of initial designs produced with Urban Launchpad in the mapping of Dhaka’s bus system to create the city’s first complete bus map.

Our goal is to enable researchers from a range of backgrounds (e.g., citizens, academics, government officials, private industry) and with a variety of purposes (understanding housing conditions, safety concerns, etc.) to create inexpensive, accurate, and robust data sets.
