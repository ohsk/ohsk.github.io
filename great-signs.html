The Good Guide to Great Signs

Illustrating New Orlean’s dense signage ordinance

Project Date
2012
Location
Broad Street, New Orleans, LA
Role
Project Designer
Organization
Broad Community Connections
Grantor
National Endowment for the Arts

The Good Guide to Great Signs translates New Orlean’s dense zoning ordinance into diagrams and visuals so that artists and businesses participating in the Iconic Signage Project can better understand the city’s signage guidelines.

Broad Street was recently designated as an Inner City Urban Corridor District, a new zoning district type of the City of New Orleans created with the goal of improving the visual appeal and pedestrian accessibility of major inner-city street corridors.

The new zoning district establishes a set of guidlines for commercial signage. The Good Guide to Great Signs provides local businesses and signage artists with a visualization of the zoning guidelines.

Calculating sign area

Sign area is the maximum size of the sign(s) a business is allowed to display (up to 70 ft2). It is calculated using the width of the building or tenant space (shown as A in the diagram below). If a building is A ft wide, then sign(s) can be as large as A ft2.
