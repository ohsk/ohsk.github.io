Brand Website Kickstarter

We think that “Made in Bangladesh” can mean so much more.

When was the last time you purchased a shirt and thought about the hands that made it and the journey it took to get to you? Chances are high that something you own was made in Bangladesh, the world’s second largest exporter of clothing. However, most of the time very little information is available about the conditions in which that item was made.

We’re launching a brand called New Market Goods that highlights positive approaches to garment production.

Promote good design

We want to create beautiful garments that reflect Bangladeshi heritage, and add vibrancy to the men's shirting market.

Celebrate Bangladeshi artisanship

Our inspiration comes from the vibrancy and textures seen on the ground in Bangladesh.

Improve production transparency

We are highlighting positive approaches to manufacturing in an industry that has become notorious for its extremely negative working conditions.

Increase community capacity

We aim to improve local livelihoods by returning a share of our profits directly back to the community that has been directly contributing to the project.

Our First Product

Our inaugural line features an all-cotton popover shirt. It takes cues from the punjabis frequently found on the streets of Dhaka, such as the band collar and a placket that extends only to mid-chest.

We’ve hybridized the style with more traditional button-down features: a slightly more fitted silhouette that hits at the waist and side gussets. The design itself is fairly minimal, and is really meant to highlight the textiles, which are produced in one of the last remaining hand-loom communities called Pabna, in western Bangladesh.

Kickstarter First Run

In the summer of 2014, we launched a Kickstarter campaign to support the project.

We successfully raised over $20,000, providign backers with a first chance to grab popovers featuring our first run of textiles.

Working with Deshal

We’ve partnered with Deshal, a community of local garment producers who’ve been striving to create better working conditions in their factory in Bangladesh.

Together we hope to promote good design, celebrate Bangladeshi artisanship, and improve transparency in the production of clothing, starting with our line of popover shirts.
